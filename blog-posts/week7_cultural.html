One of the most important values to me is integrity. Integrity is described as:

1. the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles; moral uprightness.
2. the state of being whole and undivided.

For all of the values I selected above (bold), I would agree/strongly agree that I try to live up to those values. I don't think I let stereotype threat, or most other social phenomenas, affect me negatively. I try my best to live a life full of integrity and in a manner that reflects my faith.

Just this week, a close friend asked me for advice on how to proceed with an upcoming (serious) talk between him and a community leader. Rather than focusing on knowing exactly how to respond to the leader, I advised him to reaffirm what would be best for him. In these situations, I feel that doing what's best for you often is the same as doing what's best for the community. When you sacrifice too much or stretch yourself too thin, it creates an unsustainable burden (and often, disdain) that negatively affects all those around you. In general, my advice tends to focus on the individual, rather than the environment.
