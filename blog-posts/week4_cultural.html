Issues in Tech

If you keep up with tech-related news, you'll soon start becoming unfazed by massive amounts of cash. WhatApp was purchased for $19B, Beats by Dre for $3B, Twitch for $1B. Snapchat is valued at $10B and companies are constantly getting $10M+ funding in the Series A, B, C, and more rounds. You lose touch with the value of money and thus goods.

While it's great that these brilliant people are providing so many people with a better experience, I would prefer to have those same minds solve larger issues, like poverty, energy efficiency, education, medicine, etc. How badly does the world need another messaging app, streaming service, headphones, or ad network? Not so much in my opinion. That's why I was very pleased to read this TechCrunch article:

--> New at Y Combinator: Startups Solving Huge Problems <--

Y Combinator is one of the most prestigious startup incubators so it's incredible to see a shift in the type of companies in it.There are companies building nuclear reactors, creating vaccines for HIV and AIDs, and quantum computing. These companies can really push the bounds for both helping advance science, technology, and society!
